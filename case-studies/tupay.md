# Case Study: Building Tupay from 0 to $35M+ in Payment Volume

**Role:** Senior Product Manager
**Company:** Eron International
**Product:** [Tupay](https://www.tupaypagos.com/) — Digital Payments Platform
**Market:** Peru & Ecuador
**Status:** Live and scaling

---

## The Problem

Peruvian businesses — from e-commerce to enterprise — had no unified, reliable way to:
- Accept digital payments across fragmented local methods (wallets, bank transfers, agents, cards)
- Disburse bulk payments to beneficiaries efficiently
- Onboard and verify merchants compliantly (KYC/KYB)
- Access real-time transaction visibility

Existing solutions were either too expensive, too narrow in payment method coverage, or required deep technical integration with no modular flexibility.

---

## My Role

I owned the product end-to-end — from initial discovery to launch and iteration. This included:

- Defining the product strategy and roadmap from scratch
- Leading user research with merchants, finance teams, and end consumers
- Writing specs for all core modules (pay-ins, pay-outs, KYC, dashboard, integrations)
- Coordinating engineering, design, legal/compliance, and commercial teams
- Setting and tracking OKRs across acquisition, activation, and volume metrics

## What We Built

### Core Platform
| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Tu Multipay** | Multi-method payment processing — one integration, 30+ methods |
| **Tu Payplus** | Quick collection via QR and payment links |
| **Tu Link** | Shareable payment links for non-technical sellers |
| **Tu Payout** | Bulk disbursements — daily, immediate bank transfers |
| **Tu Verify** | KYC/KYB verification for merchant onboarding |
| **Tu Plugin** | E-commerce plugin (WooCommerce, custom APIs) |
| **Tu Panel** | Real-time financial dashboard for merchants and ops |

### Payment Methods Integrated
- Digital wallets: Yape, PLIN (dominant in Peru)
- Bank transfers (online banking)
- Credit & debit cards
- Agent/bodega network (120K+ physical points)
- QR codes

## Key Outcomes

- **$35M+ USD** processed through the platform
- **120,000+** payment points across Peru
- **30+ payment methods** supported under a single API
- Expanded to Ecuador as a second market
- Triple-digit volume growth year-over-year

---

## Key Decisions & Learnings

**1. Agent network first, not cards first**
In Peru, cash-to-digital conversion happens at bodegas (corner stores). Prioritizing the agent network over card processing was counterintuitive but critical for market fit. We reached 120K+ points.

**2. Modular product architecture**
Building Tupay as a suite of named modules (Tu Multipay, Tu Verify, etc.) rather than a monolith allowed us to sell individual solutions to different customer segments and upsell over time.

**3. Compliance as a product feature**
KYC/KYB wasn't just a legal checkbox — we built Tu Verify as a merchant-facing product that reduced onboarding friction and became a selling point for enterprise clients.

**4. Spec-Driven Development**
I used detailed product specs as the primary artifact to align engineering, legal, and commercial teams — reducing back-and-forth and accelerating delivery cycles significantly.
